Poblacion

***Poblacion*** or ***población*** (literally "town" or "population" [[CITE|1|https://translate.google.com.ph/?hl=en&tab=wT#es/en/poblacion]] in [[LINK|lang_en|Spanish_language_in_the_Philippines|Spanish]]; Tagalog pronunciation: [pobläˈʃo̞n] ) is the common term used for the central, [[LINK|lang_en|Downtown|downtown]], [[LINK|lang_en|Old_town|old town]] or [[LINK|lang_en|Central_business_district|central business district]] area of a [[LINK|lang_en|Philippines|Philippine]] [[LINK|lang_en|Cities_in_the_Philippines|city]] or [[LINK|lang_en|Philippine_municipality|municipality]], which may take up the area of a single [[LINK|lang_en|Barangay|barangay]] or multiple barangays. It is sometimes shortened to "Pob."

History

During the [[LINK|lang_en|History_of_the_Philippines_(1521–1898)|Spanish rule]], the colonial government adopted the [[LINK|lang_en|Reducción_in_the_Philippines|Reducción]] policy. This [[LINK|lang_en|Population_transfer|resettlement]] process coerced inhabitants of far-flung scattered [[LINK|lang_en|Ancient_barangay|ancient barangays]] to move into a centralized *cabecera* (town/district capital) where a newly built church was situated. [[CITE|2|https://openlibrary.org/books/OL9180911M/Vol._1_The_Philippines_A_Past_Revisited_(The_Philippines_A_Past_Revisited_Volume_1)]] This allowed the Spanish government to control the movement of the indigenous population, to easily facilitate [[LINK|lang_en|Christianization|Christianization]], to conduct [[LINK|lang_en|Census|population counts]], and to collect [[LINK|lang_en|Tax|tributes]].

Features

The *población* is considered the commercial and industrial center of the city or municipality. Most citizens of a city or municipality residing in the outlying barangays and satellite *[[LINK|lang_en|Sitio|sitios]]* flock to the *población* on market days (which is set by a [[LINK|lang_en|Local_ordinance|local ordinance]] of the [[LINK|lang_en|Local_government|local government]] ) because most local products and goods from the [[LINK|lang_en|Barrio|barrios]] are brought to the public market located in the *población*. In this way, their products could be sold faster by a wide range of buyers, though there are instances where some citizens would choose to go to another town's *poblacion* because it is closer to their residence. In some cities and towns, the *población* (usually the areas surrounding the parish church) doubles as the [[LINK|lang_en|Old_town|old town]] district which features [[LINK|lang_en|Ancestral_houses_of_the_Philippines|a few remaining Spanish-built structures in the country]].

The *cabecera* (or the *población* of a *municipio* / *pueblo* ) has a basic plan, with a *[[LINK|lang_en|Plaza|plaza mayor]]*, church and attached *[[LINK|lang_en|Parish_house|convento]]*, civic buildings such as the [[LINK|lang_en|Town_hall|town hall]], and houses of prominent Spaniards. [[CITE|3|https://books.google.com.ph/books?id=xiOQdEzgP9kC]] [[CITE|5|http://tabonwoman.blogspot.com/2009/05/spanish-period.html]] Other features include the public [[LINK|lang_en|Market_(place)|market]], the central [[LINK|lang_en|Elementary_school|elementary school]] and [[LINK|lang_en|High_school|high school]], Police Station, and Hospital.
